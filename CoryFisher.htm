Sensei Cory Fisher
Cory Fisher, the owner and director of Cory's Karate, currently holds a 6th Degree Black Belt in Tan Ren Do Karate. She also holds a 5th Degree Black Belt in Karate Shin-Toshi and Tae Kwon Do Moo Duk Kwan.

Background
Sensei Fisher has been involved in martial arts since attending college. She originally started taking classes in the Kenpo style from Mr. Bobby Lawrence at Bobby Lawrence Karate in Provo, Utah and continued her training in the Kenpo style at Troy Black Belt Academy in Troy, Michigan. The close and practical techniques taught in the Kenpo style were a good fit and the experiences gained through this instruction influenced her future martial arts choices. Sensei Fisher then continued experimenting with several other martial arts styles - most notably Shotokan Karate and Tae Kwon Do. Next, Sensei Fisher continued her instruction at Utah Karate Institute in Kaysville, Utah. Sensei Fisher embraced the style of Karate Shin-Toshi with it's combination of strong techniques and dynamic kicks. Sensei Fisher trained under Kwanjang Mickey Fisher and Sensei Mike Burke in the Karate Shin-Toshi style for a period of seventeen years. Through her experiences in the martial arts, Sensei Fisher finally adopted the Tan Ren Do Karate style which includes strong influences from Karate Shin-Toshi, Kenpo, Tae Kwon Do and Shotokan Karate.

Cory's Karate
 In 2003, Sensei Fisher opened her school, Cory's Karate. Sensei Fisher is a strong proponent of the martial arts. Studying Tan Ren Do Karate is about developing the person as a whole. Learning kicking and punching is a byproduct of a martial arts education. The character development that occurs when learning martial arts is amazing and surpasses most expectations. Sensei Fisher is excited to be able to help provide that experience and growth to her students.
