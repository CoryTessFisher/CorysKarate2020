Forms: Beginner
One common “legend” concerning the tradition of belts claims that early martial artists began their training with a white belt, which eventually became stained black from years of sweat, dirt, and blood. However, there is no real evidence for this story, so it should be relegated to the status of myth. In fact, given the standard of cleanliness common in the traditional karate dojo, a student arriving with a bloodied or dirty uniform would not have been allowed to train.

Nevertheless, in the pursuit of a black belt everyone has to begin somewhere...and that rank is white belt. By diligently practicing the techniques taught in these beginning forms you will over time master the material and yourself.
